Using LightStrips Instead of Bulbs

Menu

There are times when a bulb may not be the best fit for illuminating your radio. VocoLinc also makes LightStrips that are great for these situations.

Above you can see my RCA Model 60. With the lid open, it gives off a soft warm glow that you might see if the tubes were functional.

Of course, we're faking it! In reality I just wove the lightstrip around the inside of the radio. The power cord goes out a convenient (and already existing) hole in the back.

 

During the day I can set it to a bright white, max power to really give off some extra light.

 

I can even go crazy, and set it to a wild green for example. Totally unrealistic, but fun.

 

My RCA Model 60 has a small opening for the dial, that in the radio was indeed lit. I was able to emulate that glow, so even if the lid is closed I get the illusion of a working radio. It also makes a nifty nightlight for those 3 am bathroom trips! (Hey, what can I say, I'm an old man LOL).

 

I accomplished this by simply taking a section of the lightstrip and placing it right behind the dial opening, just above the dial.

As you work on your own radios, be alert for small touches like this that can enhance the look and appeal of your radio.
