Atwater Kent Model 60

Years of Production: 1929-1930

The Atwater Kent Model 60 is a classic radio from the late 1920s, known for its robust design and high-quality performance. It was a popular choice among consumers during its time due to its reliability and advanced features.

Technical Specifications

  • Type: AC-powered tabletop radio
  • Frequency Range: AM broadcast band
  • Tubes: 9 tubes, including RF amplifiers, detector, and audio output stages
  • Speaker: Electrodynamic speaker with a rich, warm sound
  • Power Supply: Built-in power transformer

Notable Features

  • High-quality wooden cabinet with a classic design
  • Advanced circuitry for improved sensitivity and selectivity
  • Large tuning dial for precise station selection
  • Durable components designed for long-term use

Historical Context

The Atwater Kent Model 60 was introduced during a time when radio was becoming a central part of American households. It represented a shift towards more sophisticated and reliable home entertainment devices. Atwater Kent was a leading manufacturer of radios during this era, known for their innovation and craftsmanship.

Additional Details

The Model 60 is often sought after by collectors and enthusiasts due to its historical significance and elegant design. Restored units are prized for their ability to still function and provide a glimpse into the early days of radio technology.
