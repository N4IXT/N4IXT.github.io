Atwater Kent Model 30 Radio

Years of Production: 1925

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 6 (UX201A, UX201A, UX201A, UX201A or UX200 or UX112, UX201A, UX171 or UX112)
  • Main Principle: TRF without regeneration; 2 AF stages
  • Tuned Circuits: 3 AM circuits
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Storage and/or dry batteries
  • Loudspeaker: Requires external speaker
  • Material: Wooden case
  • Dimensions: 20 x 6.5 x 6.5 inches (508 x 165 x 165 mm)
  • Weight: 12 lb 11 oz (5.760 kg)
  • Price in First Year of Sale: $85

Notable Features

  • Condenser end plates molded
  • Three separate UV-sockets for RF stages
  • One dial (primary tuning control knob)
  • Metal panel and L-shaped chassis

Historical Context

The Atwater Kent Model 30 was introduced in 1925 during a period of rapid growth in the radio industry. Atwater Kent was already a well-established name, known for producing high-quality radios. The Model 30 was designed to be more user-friendly compared to previous models, featuring a single dial for tuning instead of multiple dials. This made it easier for users to operate and tune into their favorite broadcasts.

The Model 30 was part of Atwater Kent's efforts to simplify radio operation and make it more accessible to the general public. Its introduction helped solidify Atwater Kent's reputation as a leading manufacturer in the radio industry.
