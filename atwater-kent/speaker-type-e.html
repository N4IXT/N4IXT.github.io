Atwater Kent Type E Speaker

Years of Production: 1925-1929

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Magnetic cone speaker
  • Impedance: Approximately 2000 ohms
  • Material: Metal frame with a paper cone
  • Dimensions: 11 inches in diameter

Notable Features:

  • Elegant design with a decorative grille
  • Highly sensitive for its time
  • Compatible with early battery-powered radios

Historical Context:

The Atwater Kent Type E speaker was a popular accessory for radios in the late 1920s. It was designed to provide clear and loud audio output, making it a significant improvement over earlier horn speakers. Its stylish appearance made it a desirable addition to any home radio setup.

Additional Details:

Atwater Kent was a leading manufacturer of radios and accessories during the early 20th century. The Type E speaker is a testament to the company's commitment to quality and innovation in the field of audio technology.
