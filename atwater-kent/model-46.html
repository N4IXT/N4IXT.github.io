Atwater Kent Model 46

Years of Production: 1928-1930

The Atwater Kent Model 46 is a classic radio from the late 1920s, known for its elegant design and reliable performance. It was a popular choice for households during the early days of radio broadcasting.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) Receiver
  • Power Source: 110V AC
  • Tubes: 6 tubes (including 26, 27, 71A, and others)
  • Speaker: External horn or cone speaker required
  • Frequency Range: Medium wave (AM)

Notable Features:

  • Beautiful wooden cabinet with a polished finish
  • Simple and intuitive tuning mechanism
  • Durable construction with high-quality components
  • Designed for use with external antennas for better reception

Historical Context:

The Atwater Kent Model 46 was produced during a time when radio was becoming a central part of family entertainment. It represents the transition from battery-powered radios to AC-powered models, making it more convenient for users. Atwater Kent was one of the leading manufacturers of radios during this era, known for their innovation and craftsmanship.

Additional Details:

This model is highly sought after by collectors due to its historical significance and aesthetic appeal. The Model 46 is a testament to the ingenuity of early 20th-century radio engineering and remains a cherished piece of radio history.
