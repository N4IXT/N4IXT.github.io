Hallicrafters PM-23 Speaker

Years of Production: 1939-1945

The Hallicrafters PM-23 speaker was a popular accessory designed to complement Hallicrafters shortwave receivers, particularly during the late 1930s and early 1940s. It was known for its elegant design and high-quality sound reproduction.

Technical Specifications

  • 12-inch electrodynamic speaker
  • Frequency response: 50 Hz to 10 kHz
  • Power handling: 10 watts
  • Impedance: 500 ohms
  • Field coil design for enhanced sound clarity

Notable Features

  • Art Deco-inspired design with a sleek metal grille
  • Available in two variants: one with an "H" emblem on the front grille and one without
  • Durable construction with a wooden cabinet and metal accents
  • Designed to match the aesthetic of Hallicrafters receivers

Historical Context

The PM-23 speaker was introduced during a time when shortwave radio was gaining popularity among amateur radio enthusiasts and professionals. Its design and performance made it a sought-after accessory for Hallicrafters receivers, such as the SX-28 and S-20R.

Units with "H" Emblem vs. Units Without

Units with the "H" emblem on the front grille were considered more prestigious and were often marketed as premium models. The "H" emblem symbolized the Hallicrafters brand and added a distinctive touch to the speaker's appearance. Units without the emblem were functionally identical but lacked the branding, making them slightly less collectible among enthusiasts today.

Both variants shared the same technical specifications and performance, ensuring high-quality sound reproduction regardless of the design differences.
