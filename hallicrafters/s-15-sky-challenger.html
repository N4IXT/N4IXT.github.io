Hallicrafters S-15 Sky Challenger Radio

Years of Production: 1937

Overview

The Hallicrafters S-15 Sky Challenger is a vintage commercial receiver designed for both amateur and commercial use. It was introduced in 1937 and became known for its reliable performance and robust design.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 9
  • Tube Types: 6K7, 6L7, 6C5, 6K7, 6K7, 6Q7, 6K7, 6F6G, 80
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 465 kHz
  • Tuned Circuits: 11 AM circuits
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.545-1.23 MHz, 1.18-2.85 MHz, 2.75-6.82 MHz, 6.75-16.4 MHz, 15.4-38.1 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 115 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: For headphones or external amplifier
  • Power Output: 4 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 19 x 10 x 12 inches (483 x 254 x 305 mm)
  • Weight: 30 lbs (13.6 kg)

Notable Features

The S-15 Sky Challenger includes features such as a noise limiter, CW/AM switch, and a sensitivity control. It was designed to provide reliable communication in various environments, with a focus on durability and ease of use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-15 Sky Challenger was produced during a time when reliable communication equipment was essential. Its robust design and performance made it a popular choice for both amateur radio operators and commercial users.
