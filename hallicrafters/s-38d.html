Overview

The Hallicrafters S-38D is a general coverage receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was introduced in late 1954 and became known for its reliable performance and affordability.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 5
  • Tube Types: 12SA7, 12SG7, 12SQ7, 50L6, 35Z5
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast plus more than 2 Short Wave bands
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-32 MHz in 4 ranges
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC/DC, 117 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker, 5-inch diameter
  • Power Output: 1 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 13 x 7 x 7.25 inches (330 x 178 x 184 mm)
  • Weight: 10 lbs 11 oz (4.85 kg)

Notable Features

The S-38D includes features such as a BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator) for CW (Continuous Wave) reception, a noise limiter, and a sensitivity control. It was designed to provide reliable communication across a wide range of frequencies, making it suitable for both amateur and commercial use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-38D was produced during the post-World War II era, a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity.
