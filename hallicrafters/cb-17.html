Hallicrafters CB-17 Radio

Years of Production: 1960s

The Hallicrafters CB-17 is a vintage Citizens Band (CB) radio that was popular during the 1960s. It was designed for short-distance communication and was widely used by hobbyists, truckers, and small businesses.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 27 MHz (CB Band)
  • Channels: 23 channels
  • Power Output: 5 watts (AM)
  • Modulation: Amplitude Modulation (AM)
  • Power Supply: 12V DC or 110V AC with an external power supply
  • Dimensions: Compact and portable design

Notable Features:

  • Durable metal chassis for rugged use
  • Simple and intuitive controls
  • Built-in speaker and microphone jack
  • External antenna connection for improved range

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters CB-17 was part of the CB radio boom in the mid-20th century. It was a time when CB radios became a popular means of communication for both personal and professional use. The CB-17 was known for its reliability and ease of use, making it a favorite among enthusiasts.

Additional Details:

Hallicrafters, a renowned manufacturer of amateur and CB radios, was known for producing high-quality communication equipment. The CB-17 exemplifies the company's commitment to innovation and performance during the golden age of CB radio technology.
