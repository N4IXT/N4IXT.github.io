Hallicrafters HA-4 Keyer

Years of Production: 1961–1963

Technical Specifications

  • Type: Electronic Morse Code Keyer
  • Power Supply: 115V AC, 60 Hz
  • Output: Relay contacts for transmitter keying
  • Speed Range: Approximately 10–60 WPM (Words Per Minute)
  • Controls: Speed, Weight, Dot/Space adjustment, Manual keying lever
  • Inputs: Paddle (for iambic or single-lever operation)
  • Dimensions: Approx. 6.5 x 4.5 x 7.5 inches
  • Weight: Approx. 5 lbs

Notable Features

  • Solid-state circuitry (early use of transistors in keyers)
  • Adjustable sending speed and weighting
  • Relay output for compatibility with a wide range of transmitters
  • Manual lever for hand keying or testing
  • Compact and rugged metal case

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters HA-4 was introduced in the early 1960s as one of the first commercially available electronic keyers for amateur radio operators. At a time when most operators used mechanical "bug" keyers or straight keys, the HA-4 offered improved consistency and speed for sending Morse code. Its solid-state design was innovative for the era, providing reliable operation and low maintenance compared to tube-based or mechanical alternatives.

The HA-4 was often paired with Hallicrafters transmitters and transceivers, such as the HT-32 and SX-101, and became popular among CW (Continuous Wave) enthusiasts. Its introduction marked a significant step forward in amateur radio technology, paving the way for more advanced keyers and digital modes in later years.
