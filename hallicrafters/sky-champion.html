Overview

The Hallicrafters Sky Champion, including models like the S-20 and S-20R, was a mid-priced communications receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was introduced in 1938 and became known for its reliable performance and robust design.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 8 (S-20) / 9 (S-20R)
  • Tube Types (S-20): 6K7, 6L7, 6J5, 6K7, 6Q7, 6F6, 6J5, 80
  • Tube Types (S-20R): 6SK7, 6K8, 6SK7, 6SK7, 6H6, 6J5GT, 6SQ7, 6F6G, 80
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.540-1.77 MHz, 1.72-5.4 MHz, 5.3-15.7 MHz, 15.2-44 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 117 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Electro Magnetic Dynamic LS (moving-coil with field excitation coil)
  • Power Output: 3 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 18.5 x 8.5 x 9.4 inches (470 x 215 x 240 mm)
  • Weight: 25 lbs 5.3 oz (11.5 kg)

Notable Features

The Sky Champion series featured a wide frequency range, covering essential communication bands. The S-20R model included improvements such as electrical bandspread and a more modern tube lineup compared to the original S-20.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters Sky Champion was produced during a time when reliable communication equipment was crucial, especially during World War II. Its design and performance made it a popular choice among amateur radio operators and professionals alike.
