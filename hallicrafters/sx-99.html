Hallicrafters SX-99

Years of Production: 1953 - 1955

The Hallicrafters SX-99 is a vintage shortwave receiver known for its reliability and performance during the mid-20th century. It was designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and shortwave listeners.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54 to 54 MHz in 4 bands
  • Modes: AM, CW
  • Tubes: 9 vacuum tubes
  • IF Frequency: 455 kHz
  • Audio Output: 3 watts

Notable Features:

  • Bandspread tuning for precise frequency selection
  • Built-in speaker
  • Selectable bandwidth for improved signal clarity
  • Classic Hallicrafters design with a durable metal chassis

Historical Context:

The SX-99 was introduced during the post-war era when amateur radio was gaining popularity. It was an affordable yet capable receiver, making it a favorite among hobbyists. Its design reflects the technological advancements of the time, offering good performance at a reasonable price.

Additional Details:

The SX-99 remains a sought-after collector's item today, appreciated for its historical significance and nostalgic value. Many enthusiasts restore and use these radios as a testament to their enduring quality.
