Hallicrafters S-85

Years of Production: 1954 - 1959

The Hallicrafters S-85 is a vintage shortwave receiver that was popular among amateur radio enthusiasts during the mid-20th century. It is known for its robust design and reliable performance.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54 to 34 MHz in four bands
  • Modes: AM, CW
  • Tubes: 8 vacuum tubes
  • Power Supply: 110-120V AC
  • Audio Output: 3.5 watts

Notable Features:

  • Bandspread tuning for precise frequency selection
  • Built-in speaker for convenient listening
  • Selectable AVC (Automatic Volume Control)
  • Adjustable BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator) for CW reception

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters S-85 was introduced during the golden age of shortwave radio, a time when amateur radio operators and hobbyists were exploring the world of global communication. Its affordability and solid performance made it a popular choice for beginners and seasoned operators alike. The S-85 is still cherished by collectors and enthusiasts for its classic design and historical significance.
