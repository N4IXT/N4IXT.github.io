Hallicrafters CRX-4 Civic Patrol

Years of Production: 1959-1962

The Hallicrafters CRX-4 Civic Patrol was a VHF receiver designed for public safety and civic communication purposes. It was widely used by police departments, fire services, and other municipal organizations during its production years.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 30-50 MHz (VHF low band)
  • Mode: AM (Amplitude Modulation)
  • Power Supply: 110-120V AC
  • Audio Output: 3.5 watts
  • IF Frequency: 10.7 MHz
  • Tube Complement: 6 tubes including RF amplifier, mixer, and audio output stages

Notable Features:

  • Compact and rugged design for reliable operation
  • Front-panel tuning with a large, easy-to-read dial
  • Built-in speaker for clear audio output
  • External antenna connection for improved reception

Historical Context:

The CRX-4 was introduced during a time when VHF communication was becoming increasingly important for public safety. Its affordability and reliability made it a popular choice for smaller municipalities and volunteer organizations. The radio's design reflected Hallicrafters' commitment to producing high-quality communication equipment for both amateur and professional use.

Additional Details:

The Hallicrafters CRX-4 is now considered a collector's item among vintage radio enthusiasts. Its historical significance and robust construction make it a prized possession for those interested in the evolution of communication technology.
