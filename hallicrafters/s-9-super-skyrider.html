Hallicrafters S-9 Super Skyrider Radio

Years of Production: 1936

Overview

The Hallicrafters S-9 Super Skyrider is a vintage commercial receiver designed for both amateur and commercial use. It was introduced in 1936 and became known for its reliable performance and robust design.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 9
  • Tube Types: 6K7, 6L7, 6C5, 6K7, 6H6, 6K7, 6F5, 6F6, 5Z4
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 465 kHz
  • Tuned Circuits: 8 AM circuits
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.545-1.53 MHz, 1.495-4.2 MHz, 3.950-11.5 MHz, 8.0-21.75 MHz, 20-42 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 110 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Electro Magnetic Dynamic LS (moving-coil with field excitation coil)
  • Power Output: 3.5 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 18.5 x 8.5 x 9.4 inches (470 x 215 x 240 mm)
  • Weight: 25 lbs 5.3 oz (11.5 kg)

Notable Features

The S-9 Super Skyrider was one of the first receivers to use metal tubes and iron core IF transformers. It featured a wide frequency range, covering essential communication bands, and was designed for both amateur and commercial use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-9 Super Skyrider was produced during a time when reliable communication equipment was crucial. Its robust design and performance made it a popular choice among amateur radio operators and professionals alike[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_super_skyrider_s_9_s9.html)[2](https://rigreference.com/rigs/2793-hallicrafters-s-9-super-skyrider).
