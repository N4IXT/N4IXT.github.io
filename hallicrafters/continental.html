Hallicrafters Continental Receiver

Years of Production: 1946-1948

The Hallicrafters Continental Receiver was a high-performance shortwave radio designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and professionals. It was known for its advanced features and robust build quality, making it a popular choice during its time.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 540 kHz to 30 MHz
  • Modes: AM, CW
  • Tubes: 12-tube superheterodyne design
  • Power Supply: 110-120V AC
  • Audio Output: 5 watts

Notable Features:

  • High sensitivity and selectivity for clear reception
  • Bandspread tuning for precise frequency adjustment
  • Built-in speaker with excellent audio quality
  • Durable metal chassis and classic design

Historical Context:

Produced shortly after World War II, the Hallicrafters Continental Receiver reflected the technological advancements of the era. It was widely used by amateur radio operators and played a role in fostering global communication during the post-war years. Its reliability and performance made it a collector's item among vintage radio enthusiasts today.
