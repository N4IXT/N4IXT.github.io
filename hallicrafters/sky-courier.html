Hallicrafters Sky Courier Radio

Years of Production: 1942

Overview

The Hallicrafters Sky Courier, also known as the RE-1, is a portable receiver designed for both amateur and commercial use. It was introduced in 1942 and became known for its reliable performance and robust design, especially during World War II.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 7
  • Tube Types: 1R5, 1N5GT, 1N5GT, 1H5GT, 50L6GT, 3Q5GT, 35Z5GT
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast plus 2 Short Wave bands
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-19 MHz in 3 bands
  • Power Type and Voltage: Line/Batteries (AC/DC 105-120V, 67.5V, 7.5V)
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker, 4-inch diameter
  • Power Output: 0.08 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 8 x 8 x 8 inches (203 x 203 x 203 mm)
  • Weight: 10 lbs (4.5 kg)

Notable Features

The Sky Courier was designed to be a portable set, usable without mains power. It was often used as a troop entertainer and morale booster during World War II. The receiver could operate on various power sources, including line and batteries, making it versatile for different environments.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters Sky Courier was produced during World War II, a time when reliable and portable communication equipment was essential. Its robust design and performance made it a popular choice for both military and civilian use[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_sky_courier_re_1_re1.html)[2](https://www.myantiqueradiomuseum.org/hallicrafters-radios).
