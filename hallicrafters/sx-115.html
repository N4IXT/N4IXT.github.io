Hallicrafters SX-115

Years of Production: 1961-1964

The Hallicrafters SX-115 is a high-performance communications receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It covers multiple shortwave bands and offers advanced features for its time.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 3.5-4, 7-7.5, 14-14.5, 21-21.5, 28-28.5, 28.5-29, 29-29.5, 29.5-30, 9.6-10 MHz
  • Modulation: AM
  • Receiver System: Superheterodyne, double/triple conversion
  • IF Frequencies: 6005 kHz, 1005 kHz, 50.75 kHz
  • Audio Output: 1.5 W (requires external speaker)
  • Power Requirements: 105-125 Volt AC
  • Number of Tubes: 17
  • Wave Bands: Shortwave (SW only)
  • Price in First Year of Sale: $600.00

Notable Features:

  • Superheterodyne principle with double/triple conversion for improved selectivity
  • Crystal filter for enhanced performance
  • Gear-driven tuning with virtually no backlash
  • Matches the HT-33 and HT-32 transmitters
  • Designed to compete with the Collins 75A/S series

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters SX-115 was produced during a period of rapid advancement in amateur radio technology. Hallicrafters was known for its innovative designs and high-quality craftsmanship, making the SX-115 a popular choice among serious radio enthusiasts. Its robust construction and reliable performance contributed to its reputation as a top-tier communications receiver.
