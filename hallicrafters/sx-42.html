Overview

The Hallicrafters SX-42 is a vintage communications receiver designed for both amateur and commercial use. It was introduced in 1946 and became known for its extensive band coverage and advanced features.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 15
  • Tube Types: 6AG5, 6AG5, 7F8, 6SK7, 6SG7, 6H6, 7H7, 7H7, 6H6, 6SL7, 7A4, 6V6, 6V6, VR150, 5U4
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and FM
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-1.62 MHz, 1.62-5 MHz, 5-15 MHz, 15-30 MHz, 27-55 MHz, 55-110 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 105-125 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Requires external speaker
  • Power Output: 8 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 20 x 10.2 x 16 inches (508 x 259 x 406 mm)
  • Weight: 51 lbs 15.7 oz (23.6 kg)

Notable Features

The SX-42 includes features such as a crystal filter, BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator), six-position selectivity switch, noise limiter, and continuous FM coverage from 27 to 110 MHz. It was designed to provide reliable communication across a wide range of frequencies, making it suitable for both amateur and commercial use[2](https://www.radioworld.com/tech-and-gear/happy-memory-the-hallicrafters-sx42-communications-receiver)[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_sx_42_sx42.html).

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters SX-42 was produced during the post-World War II era, a time when advanced communication equipment was in high demand. Its design reflects the technological advancements of the era and the needs of radio enthusiasts and professionals[2](https://www.radioworld.com/tech-and-gear/happy-memory-the-hallicrafters-sx42-communications-receiver)[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_sx_42_sx42.html).
