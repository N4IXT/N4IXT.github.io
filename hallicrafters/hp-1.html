Hallicrafters HP-1 Power Supply

Years of Production: 1950s

The Hallicrafters HP-1 Power Supply was designed to provide reliable power for Hallicrafters' line of amateur radio equipment. It was a key accessory for radio enthusiasts during the mid-20th century.

Technical Specifications:

  • Input Voltage: 110-120V AC
  • Output Voltage: 250V DC and 6.3V AC for tube filaments
  • Maximum Current: 100 mA
  • Rectifier: Vacuum tube-based design

Notable Features:

  • Compact and durable metal chassis
  • Integrated fuse for safety
  • Designed to complement Hallicrafters receivers and transmitters

Historical Context:

The HP-1 was introduced during the golden age of amateur radio, when Hallicrafters was a leading manufacturer of radio equipment. It was a popular choice for hobbyists who needed a dependable power supply for their tube-based radios. The HP-1's robust design and reliable performance made it a staple in many amateur radio setups of the era.

Additional Details:

The Hallicrafters HP-1 is now considered a collectible item among vintage radio enthusiasts. Its design reflects the engineering standards of the 1950s, and it remains a testament to the innovation of the time.
