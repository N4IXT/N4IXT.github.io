Overview

The Hallicrafters S-107 is a general coverage receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was introduced in 1958 as a replacement for the S-53A and became known for its reliable performance and robust design.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 8
  • Tube Types: 6BA6, 6C4, 6BA6, 6BA6, 6H6, 6SC7, 6K6, 5Y3
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast plus more than 2 Short Wave bands
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-1.63 MHz, 2.5-6.3 MHz, 6.3-16 MHz, 14-31 MHz, 48-54.5 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 105-125 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker, 5-inch diameter
  • Power Output: 1 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 13.4 x 8.9 x 7 inches (340 x 225 x 178 mm)
  • Weight: 15 lbs 13.7 oz (7.2 kg)

Notable Features

The S-107 includes features such as dual IF amplifiers, a noise limiter, and a sensitivity control. It was designed to provide reliable communication across a wide range of frequencies, making it suitable for both amateur and commercial use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-107 was produced during a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity.
