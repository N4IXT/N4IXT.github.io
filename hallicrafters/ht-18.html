Hallicrafters HT-18

Years of Production: 1947-1950

The Hallicrafters HT-18 is a vintage VFO (Variable Frequency Oscillator) transmitter designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was one of the early post-war transmitters that gained popularity for its reliability and performance.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 160 meters to 10 meters (1.8 MHz to 30 MHz)
  • Power Output: Approximately 10 watts
  • Modulation: CW (Continuous Wave) and AM (Amplitude Modulation)
  • Tubes: Utilizes a combination of vacuum tubes for signal generation and amplification
  • Power Supply: Operates on 110-120V AC

Notable Features:

  • Stable VFO for precise frequency control
  • Compact and lightweight design for its time
  • Compatible with a variety of external amplifiers and receivers
  • Front-panel controls for easy operation

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters HT-18 was introduced during the late 1940s, a period when amateur radio was experiencing significant growth following World War II. The transmitter was designed to meet the needs of amateur operators who required a reliable and affordable VFO-based transmitter. Its introduction marked a shift from crystal-controlled transmitters to more flexible VFO designs, allowing operators to tune across a wider range of frequencies.

Additional Details:

The HT-18 is often paired with Hallicrafters receivers such as the SX-28 or SX-42 to create a complete amateur radio station. Collectors and enthusiasts value the HT-18 for its historical significance and its role in advancing amateur radio technology during the mid-20th century.
