Overview

The Hallicrafters HT-32 is a vintage amateur radio transmitter known for its robust design and reliable performance. It was produced during the late 1950s and became popular among amateur radio enthusiasts.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 18
  • Tube Types: 12AX7, 12AX7, 6AL5, 12AT7, 12AU7, 12AU7, 6AH6, 6AH6, 12AT7, 6AU6, 6AB4, 6AH6, 6AH6, 6CB6, 12BY7, 6146, 6146, 5R4GY, 0A2
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne
  • Wave Bands: 80 to 10 meters
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 105-125 Volt
  • Output Power: 70-100 watts CW/SSB, 17-25 watts AM
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 20 x 10.5 x 17 inches (508 x 267 x 432 mm)
  • Weight: 85 lbs 14.4 oz (39 kg)

Notable Features

The HT-32 includes features such as full band switching, metering, filter-type SSB, CW break-in keying, VOX, TVI suppression, calibrated VFO, and a built-in cooling fan. It matches well with the HT-33 and SX-101 receivers[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_ht_32_ht32.html)[2](https://www.eham.net/reviews/view-product?id=1684).

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters HT-32 was produced during a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity. Its design reflects the technological advancements of the era and the needs of amateur radio operators[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_ht_32_ht32.html)[2](https://www.eham.net/reviews/view-product?id=1684).
