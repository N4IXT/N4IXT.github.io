Hallicrafters S-29 Receiver

Years of Production: 1939-1941

The Hallicrafters S-29, also known as the "Sky Traveler," was a portable shortwave receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and general shortwave listeners. It was one of the early models that combined portability with advanced features for its time.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 540 kHz to 30 MHz in four bands
  • Tubes: 6-tube superheterodyne circuit
  • Power Supply: Operates on AC or DC
  • Audio Output: Built-in speaker and headphone jack
  • Dimensions: Compact and portable design

Notable Features:

  • Bandspread tuning for precise frequency selection
  • Portable design with a built-in handle
  • Dual power capability for flexibility
  • Durable metal case for rugged use

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters S-29 was introduced just before World War II, during a time when shortwave listening was gaining popularity. Its portability and performance made it a favorite among hobbyists and travelers. The receiver was also used by military personnel and emergency services during the war, showcasing its reliability and versatility.

Additional Details:

The S-29 was part of Hallicrafters' effort to make shortwave radios more accessible to the general public. Its combination of affordability, portability, and performance helped establish Hallicrafters as a leading name in the radio industry during the mid-20th century.
