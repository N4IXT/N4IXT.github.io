Hallicrafters CRX-1 Radio

Years of Production: 1950s

The Hallicrafters CRX-1 is a vintage shortwave radio receiver that was popular in the 1950s. It was designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and offered a range of features that made it suitable for both casual listening and serious DXing (long-distance reception).

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 1.5 MHz to 30 MHz
  • Tuning: Continuous tuning with a variable capacitor
  • IF Frequency: 455 kHz
  • Power Supply: 110V AC
  • Dimensions: 12 x 8 x 6 inches

Notable Features:

  • High sensitivity and selectivity for clear signal reception
  • Built-in speaker for convenient audio output
  • Durable metal chassis with a classic design
  • Easy-to-read dial with calibrated frequency markings

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters CRX-1 was introduced during a time when shortwave radio was becoming increasingly popular among hobbyists and professionals. It was part of Hallicrafters' lineup, a company known for its high-quality amateur radio equipment. The CRX-1 was particularly favored by radio enthusiasts for its performance and reliability.

Additional Details:

The CRX-1 is now considered a collector's item, valued for its historical significance and vintage appeal. Many enthusiasts restore these radios to their original condition, preserving a piece of radio history.
