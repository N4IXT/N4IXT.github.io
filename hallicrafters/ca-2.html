Hallicrafters CA-2 Skyfone

Years of Production: 1947-1949

The Hallicrafters CA-2 Skyfone is a vintage AM transceiver designed for mobile and fixed-station use. It was primarily marketed to amateur radio operators and aviation enthusiasts during the late 1940s.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 2.5 to 6 MHz (AM mode)
  • Power Output: Approximately 10 watts
  • Power Supply: 6V DC or 110V AC with an external power supply
  • Modulation: Amplitude Modulation (AM)
  • Tubes: Utilizes 6 vacuum tubes for operation

Notable Features:

  • Compact and portable design for its time
  • Integrated microphone and speaker
  • Simple tuning mechanism for ease of use
  • Durable metal chassis for rugged environments

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters CA-2 Skyfone was introduced shortly after World War II, during a period of rapid innovation in radio technology. It was designed to meet the needs of amateur radio operators who required reliable communication equipment for both personal and emergency use. The CA-2 was also popular among aviation enthusiasts due to its portability and ease of operation.

Additional Details:

While the CA-2 Skyfone is no longer in production, it remains a sought-after collector's item among vintage radio enthusiasts. Its historical significance and unique design make it a valuable piece of amateur radio history.
