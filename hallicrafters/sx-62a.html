Hallicrafters SX-62A

Years of Production: 1948 - 1953

The Hallicrafters SX-62A is a high-performance general coverage receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and shortwave listeners. It is renowned for its exceptional audio quality and wide frequency coverage.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 540 kHz to 108 MHz (AM/FM/Shortwave)
  • Modes: AM, FM, CW
  • Intermediate Frequency (IF): 10.7 MHz (FM), 455 kHz (AM)
  • Audio Output: Push-pull 6V6 tubes for high-fidelity sound
  • Tuning: Continuous bandspread tuning
  • Power Supply: 110-120V AC

Notable Features:

  • Built-in speaker with high-quality audio output
  • Dual conversion for improved selectivity and sensitivity
  • Large, easy-to-read tuning dial with band indicators
  • Elegant design with a robust metal chassis

Historical Context:

The SX-62A was introduced during the post-World War II era, a time when amateur radio and shortwave listening were gaining popularity. Its advanced features and superior audio performance made it a favorite among radio enthusiasts. The inclusion of FM reception was particularly notable, as FM broadcasting was still in its early stages of adoption.

Additional Details:

The SX-62A is often praised for its versatility, allowing users to listen to everything from local AM/FM broadcasts to international shortwave stations. Its combination of technical innovation and aesthetic appeal has made it a sought-after collector's item among vintage radio enthusiasts.
