Overview

The Hallicrafters S-40 is a general coverage short-wave receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was introduced in 1946 and became known for its reliable performance and robust design.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 9
  • Tube Types: 6SG7, 6SA7, 6SK7, 6SK7, 6SQ7, 6F6, 6H6, 6J5, 80
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-1.7 MHz, 1.7-5.35 MHz, 5.35-15.7 MHz, 15.7-43 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC, 117 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker, 5-inch diameter
  • Power Output: 2.5 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 18.5 x 9 x 11 inches (470 x 229 x 279 mm)
  • Weight: 28 lbs (12.7 kg)

Notable Features

The S-40 includes features such as a noise limiter, CW/AM switch, and a sensitivity control. It was designed to provide reliable communication across a wide range of frequencies, making it suitable for both amateur and commercial use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-40 was produced during the post-World War II era, a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity. Its design reflects the technological advancements of the era and the needs of radio enthusiasts[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_s_40_s40_1.html)[2](http://www.heinemoradio.se/hallicrafters-s-40/).
