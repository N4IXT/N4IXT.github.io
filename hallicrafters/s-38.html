Overview

The Hallicrafters S-38 is a general coverage receiver designed for amateur radio enthusiasts. It was introduced in 1946 and became known for its reliable performance and affordability.

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 6
  • Tube Types: 12SA7, 12SK7, 12SQ7GT, 12SQ7GT, 35L6GT, 35Z5GT
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with IF frequency 455 kHz
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Frequency Coverage: 0.54-1.65 MHz, 1.65-5.0 MHz, 5.0-14.5 MHz, 13.5-32 MHz
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC/DC, 105-120 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker, 5-inch diameter
  • Power Output: 1.6 W
  • Material: Metal case
  • Dimensions: 13 x 6.7 x 7.7 inches (330 x 170 x 195 mm)
  • Weight: 11 lbs 7.3 oz (5.2 kg)

Notable Features

The S-38 includes features such as a BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator) for CW (Continuous Wave) reception, a noise limiter, and a sensitivity control. It was designed to provide reliable communication across a wide range of frequencies, making it suitable for both amateur and commercial use.

Historical Context

The Hallicrafters S-38 was produced during the post-World War II era, a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity. Its design reflects the technological advancements of the era and the needs of radio enthusiasts[1](https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/hallicraft_s_38_s38_1.html)[2](https://people.ohio.edu/postr/bapix/S38.htm).
