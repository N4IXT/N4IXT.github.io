Hallicrafters S-10 Ultra Skyrider

Years of Production: 1936-1938

The Hallicrafters S-10 Ultra Skyrider was a groundbreaking shortwave receiver introduced in the mid-1930s. It was designed for amateur radio enthusiasts and professionals who required high-performance equipment during the golden age of radio communication.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 1.5 MHz to 30 MHz
  • Tubes: 9 vacuum tubes for enhanced performance
  • IF Frequency: 465 kHz
  • Power Supply: 110-120V AC
  • Audio Output: 2.5 watts

Notable Features:

  • High sensitivity and selectivity for clear signal reception
  • Bandspread tuning for precise frequency adjustments
  • Beautifully designed metal chassis with a durable finish
  • Built-in speaker for convenient audio output

Historical Context:

The S-10 Ultra Skyrider was introduced during a time when shortwave radio was becoming increasingly popular for both hobbyists and professionals. Its advanced features and reliable performance made it a favorite among early radio operators. The radio played a significant role in advancing communication technology and remains a cherished piece of history for collectors and enthusiasts today.

Additional Details:

The Hallicrafters S-10 Ultra Skyrider is often regarded as a symbol of innovation in the early days of radio. Its robust design and exceptional performance set a standard for future receivers, and it continues to be a sought-after item for vintage radio collectors.
