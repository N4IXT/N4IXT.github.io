Hallicrafters SX-104

Years of Production: 1957-1958

The Hallicrafters SX-104 is a desktop VHF receiver covering 30-50 MHz. It features continuous tuning steps and a single conversion receiver system with an IF frequency of 10.700 MHz. Audio output is provided by a built-in speaker.

Key Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 30-50 MHz
  • Modulation: AM
  • Receiver System: Single conversion
  • IF Frequency: 10.700 MHz
  • Audio Output: Built-in speaker
  • Power Requirements: Mains
  • Number of Tubes: 8
  • Wave Bands: AM Reception
  • Price in First Year of Sale: $90.00

Notable Features:

  • Superheterodyne principle with a crystal filter for improved selectivity
  • Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker for enhanced audio quality
  • Metal case for durability

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters SX-104 was produced during a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity. Hallicrafters was known for its innovative designs and quality craftsmanship, making the SX-104 a sought-after model among radio enthusiasts. Its robust construction and reliable performance contributed to its reputation as a dependable VHF receiver.
