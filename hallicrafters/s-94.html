Hallicrafters S-94 Transceiver

Years of Production: 1955-1957

The Hallicrafters S-94, also known as the "Sky Courier," was a compact AM/CW transceiver designed for amateur radio operators. It was primarily intended for mobile and portable use, making it a popular choice for operators on the go.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: 80, 40, 20, and 10 meters amateur bands
  • Modes: AM (Amplitude Modulation) and CW (Continuous Wave)
  • Power Output: Approximately 10 watts
  • Power Supply: Operates on 6V DC or 110V AC with an external power supply
  • Tubes: Utilizes vacuum tube technology
  • Dimensions: Compact and lightweight for portability

Notable Features:

  • Designed for mobile and portable operation
  • Simple and user-friendly controls
  • Built-in speaker for convenient monitoring
  • Durable construction suitable for field use

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters S-94 was introduced during the mid-1950s, a time when amateur radio was gaining popularity among hobbyists and professionals alike. Its portability and ease of use made it a favorite among operators who needed a reliable transceiver for mobile or emergency communication. The S-94 represents a significant step in the evolution of amateur radio equipment, bridging the gap between stationary and mobile operation.

Additional Details:

Today, the Hallicrafters S-94 is considered a collector's item among vintage radio enthusiasts. Its historical significance and unique design make it a sought-after piece for those interested in the golden age of amateur radio.
