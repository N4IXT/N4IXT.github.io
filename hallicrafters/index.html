Overview

The Hallicrafters Corporation was a prominent American manufacturer of radio equipment, founded by William J. Halligan in Chicago, Illinois, in 1932. The company quickly gained a reputation for producing high-quality amateur radio receivers and transmitters, as well as commercial and military communication equipment.

Key Milestones

  • 1932: Founded by William J. Halligan.
  • 1933: Acquired Silver-Marshall Inc., gaining an RCA patent license.
  • 1935: Introduced the SX-9 'Super Skyrider', their first line of communications receivers.
  • 1938: Expanded business to 89 countries and began producing radio transmitters.
  • 1940s: Contributed significantly to World War II efforts with designs like the HT-4/BC-610.
  • 1945-1963: Boom years, producing highly regarded equipment such as the S-38 receiver.
  • 1966: Sold to Northrop Corporation; Halligan family involvement ended.
  • 1975: Northrop sold the company name, ending non-military electronics production.

Notable Products

Hallicrafters was known for a range of innovative products, including:

  • SX-28 Super Skyrider
  • S-38 Receiver
  • HT-4/BC-610 Transmitter
  • SX-88 Receiver

Historical Context

During its peak, Hallicrafters was one of the "Big Three" manufacturers of shortwave and amateur radio equipment, alongside National Radio Company and Hammarlund. The company's contributions during World War II earned it Army-Navy 'E' Awards for excellence in production. Post-war, Hallicrafters continued to innovate, producing consumer electronics such as televisions and phonographs.

Legacy

Although Hallicrafters ceased non-military production in 1975, its legacy lives on among radio enthusiasts and collectors. The company's equipment is still highly regarded for its design, performance, and historical significance.
