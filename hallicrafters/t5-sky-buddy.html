Hallicrafters Sky Buddy T5 Radio

Years of Production: 1935-1936

Technical Specifications:

  • Number of Tubes: 5
  • Tube Types: 6A7, 6F7, 75, 42, 80
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne (common); ZF/IF 465 kHz; 2 AF stages
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast, Short Wave, and Police
  • Power Type and Voltage: AC / 110-120 Volt
  • Dimensions: 17 x 7.5 x 8.6 inches (432 x 191 x 218 mm)
  • Material: Metal case

Notable Features:

  • Coverage: 0.545-1.68, 1.68-5.5, 5.5-16 MHz
  • BFO (Beat Frequency Oscillator)
  • Two different dial designs
  • Electro Magnetic Dynamic Loudspeaker

Historical Context:

The Hallicrafters Sky Buddy T5 was introduced during the Great Depression as an affordable option for amateur radio enthusiasts. Priced at $29.50, it was designed to be accessible to the general public, with payment plans available. The radio played a significant role in popularizing the amateur radio hobby, which could lead to future purchases of more advanced equipment. The name "Sky Buddy" was inspired by a neighbor's son named Buddy, who wanted a shortwave radio but couldn't afford one.
