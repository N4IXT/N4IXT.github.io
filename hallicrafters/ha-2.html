Hallicrafters HA-2 Transverter

Years of Production: 1959-1962

The Hallicrafters HA-2 is a 2-meter transverter designed to convert signals between the 2-meter amateur radio band and the HF band, allowing operators to use their HF radios for VHF communication.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Range: 144-148 MHz (2-meter band)
  • Input Frequency: 28-30 MHz (10-meter band)
  • Power Output: Approximately 10 watts
  • Mode: AM and CW
  • Intermediate Frequency (IF): 10.7 MHz
  • Tubes: Utilizes vacuum tube technology

Notable Features:

  • Compact design for easy integration with HF radios.
  • High-quality construction typical of Hallicrafters equipment.
  • Designed for use with Hallicrafters HF transmitters like the HT-32.

Historical Context:

The HA-2 was introduced during the late 1950s, a period of rapid growth in amateur VHF operation. It allowed amateur radio operators to explore the 2-meter band without needing a dedicated VHF transceiver, making it a popular choice for enthusiasts transitioning to VHF communication.

Hallicrafters was a leading manufacturer of amateur radio equipment during this era, and the HA-2 exemplified their commitment to innovation and quality. The transverter played a significant role in expanding the capabilities of amateur radio operators and remains a cherished piece of vintage equipment among collectors today.
