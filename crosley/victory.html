Crosley Victory Radio

Years of Production: 1942-1945

The Crosley Victory Radio was produced during World War II as part of the war effort. It was designed to be a reliable and affordable radio for American households during a time of resource scarcity.

Technical Specifications

  • Type: AM Broadcast Receiver
  • Power Supply: AC/DC
  • Tube Count: 5
  • Frequency Range: 540 kHz - 1600 kHz
  • Wooden Cabinet with a simple, utilitarian design

Notable Features

  • Compact and lightweight design
  • Durable construction to withstand wartime conditions
  • Affordable price point for the average American family

Historical Context

The Crosley Victory Radio was named to reflect the patriotic spirit of the time. During World War II, many consumer goods were rationed or repurposed for the war effort. Radios like the Crosley Victory were designed to use fewer materials while still providing essential communication and entertainment for families.

Crosley was a well-known manufacturer of radios and other consumer electronics, and the Victory model became a symbol of resilience and ingenuity during a challenging period in history.
