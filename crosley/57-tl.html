Crosley 57-TL Radio

Years of Production: 1947-1948

The Crosley 57-TL is a classic tube radio produced in the late 1940s. It is a testament to the post-war era of American manufacturing, combining functionality with stylish design.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: AM Broadcast Receiver
  • Power Source: AC (Alternating Current)
  • Tubes: 5 (12SA7, 12SK7, 12SQ7, 50L6GT, 35Z5GT)
  • Speaker: Built-in 4-inch electrodynamic speaker
  • Cabinet: Bakelite construction

Notable Features:

  • Compact and lightweight design for its time
  • Art Deco-inspired styling with a streamlined Bakelite cabinet
  • Simple and reliable circuitry for consistent performance
  • Illuminated tuning dial for easy station selection

Historical Context:

The Crosley 57-TL was introduced during a period of rapid technological advancement and economic growth in the United States. Radios like the 57-TL were affordable and widely available, making them a staple in many American households. Crosley was known for producing innovative and budget-friendly radios, helping to bring entertainment and news to the masses.

Additional Details:

The Bakelite cabinet of the Crosley 57-TL is highly collectible today, as it represents the industrial design trends of the 1940s. Many enthusiasts and collectors seek out this model for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance.
