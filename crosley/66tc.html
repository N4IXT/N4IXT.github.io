Crosley Model 66TC

Years of Production: 1946-1947

Technical Specifications

  • Type: AM/SW Table Radio
  • Tubes: 6 (12SA7, 12SK7, 12SQ7, 35Z5GT, 50L6GT, 12SG7)
  • Frequency Range: AM (540-1700 kHz), Shortwave (5.8-18 MHz)
  • Power Supply: AC (110-120V)
  • Speaker: Permanent magnet dynamic (PM), approx. 4-6 inches
  • Cabinet: Bakelite or wood, with Art Deco styling

Notable Features

  • Dual-band reception (AM and Shortwave)
  • Illuminated dial with clear frequency markings
  • Classic postwar design with streamlined curves
  • Push-button tuning (on some variants)

Historical Context

The Crosley 66TC was introduced shortly after World War II, during a period of renewed consumer demand for home electronics. Crosley, a well-known American manufacturer, designed this model to be both affordable and stylish, appealing to families eager for news and entertainment. Its dual-band capability allowed listeners to tune into both local AM stations and international shortwave broadcasts, making it a versatile choice for the era. It was often referred to as the "American Overseas" model due to the design of the dial.

Additional Details

  • Dimensions: Approx. 12 x 8 x 7 inches
  • Original price: Around $30 (late 1940s USD)
  • Collectibility: Sought after by vintage radio enthusiasts for its design and reliable performance
