RCA Aeriola Sr.

Years of Production: 1922-1924

The RCA Aeriola Sr. was a high-quality regenerative receiver designed for early radio enthusiasts. It was one of the first commercially available radios that allowed users to tune into AM broadcasts with precision.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Regenerative Receiver
  • Frequency Range: AM Broadcast Band
  • Power Source: Battery-operated
  • Vacuum Tube: Single triode tube
  • Audio Output: Headphones

Notable Features:

  • Compact and elegant wooden cabinet design
  • Regenerative circuit for improved sensitivity and selectivity
  • Simple tuning mechanism for ease of use

Historical Context:

The RCA Aeriola Sr. was introduced during the early 1920s, a time when radio technology was rapidly advancing. It played a significant role in making radio more accessible to the general public, marking the transition from experimental setups to consumer-grade devices. The Aeriola Sr. is considered a collector's item today due to its historical significance and craftsmanship.

Additional Details:

The Aeriola Sr. was often paired with high-impedance headphones and required careful adjustment of the regeneration control to avoid oscillation. Its design set the standard for many radios that followed in the 1920s.
