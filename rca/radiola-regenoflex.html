RCA Radiola Regenoflex Radio

Years of Production: 1924

Technical Specifications:

  • Number of Tubes: 4
  • Tube Types: WD-11
  • Main Principle: TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) with regeneration
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Dry batteries
  • Dimensions: 17 x 7.5 x 8.6 inches (432 x 191 x 218 mm)
  • Material: Wooden case

Notable Features:

  • Two primary tuning control knobs
  • External speaker or headphones required
  • Tuned circuits: 1 AM circuit
  • Coverage: 180 to 550 meters

Historical Context:

The RCA Radiola Regenoflex, also known as the Radiola AR-817, was introduced in 1924. It was a variation of the Radiola X, housed in a more affordable cabinet. The Regenoflex utilized the TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) principle with regeneration, which was a common design in early radio technology. Priced at $150.00, it was aimed at radio enthusiasts and early adopters of radio technology. The Regenoflex played a significant role in the early days of broadcast radio, helping to popularize radio listening in the United States.
