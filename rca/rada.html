RCA RADA Radio

Years of Production: 1921

Technical Specifications:

  • Number of Tubes: 3
  • Tube Types: UV200 or UV201
  • Main Principle: TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) with regeneration
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Storage and/or dry batteries
  • Dimensions: 13.5 x 9.5 x 8.5 inches (343 x 241 x 216 mm)
  • Material: Wooden case

Notable Features:

  • First commercial receiver sold by RCA
  • External speaker required
  • Tuned circuits: 1 AM circuit
  • Coverage: 180 to 700 meters

Historical Context:

The RCA RADA radio, also known as the Westinghouse RC, was the first commercial receiver sold by RCA. It combined the RA and DA chassis into a single common box, making it a significant innovation in early radio technology. The radio was designed for use with headphones or an external amplifier, and it played a crucial role in the early days of broadcast radio. Priced at $133.00 in its first year of sale, the RADA radio was a premium product aimed at enthusiasts and early adopters of radio technology.
