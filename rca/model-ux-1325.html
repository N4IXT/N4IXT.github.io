RCA UX-1325 Speaker

Years of Production: 1930s

The RCA UX-1325 speaker was a popular accessory during the early 1930s, designed to complement RCA's line of radios. It featured a stylish Art Deco design that matched the aesthetic trends of the era.

Early ads often showed the UX-1325 being sold as a bundle with the RCA Super Hetrodyne radio.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Electrodynamic speaker
  • Impedance: 2000 ohms
  • Frequency Response: 100 Hz - 5 kHz
  • Power Handling: 5 watts

Notable Features:

  • Elegant wooden cabinet with a fabric grille
  • Compact design for tabletop use
  • High-quality sound reproduction for its time

Historical Context:

The RCA UX-1325 speaker was introduced during a period when radio was becoming a central part of home entertainment. Its design and performance reflected the technological advancements of the time, making it a sought-after accessory for radio enthusiasts.

Additional Details:

This speaker is now considered a collector's item, valued for its historical significance and unique design. Restored units are often displayed in vintage radio collections or used as decorative pieces in retro-themed interiors.
