RCA Radiola III Radio

Years of Production: 1924-1926

Technical Specifications:

  • Number of Tubes: 2
  • Tube Types: WD-11
  • Main Principle: TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) with regeneration
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Dry batteries
  • Dimensions: 7.9 x 5.9 x 6.7 inches (200 x 150 x 170 mm)
  • Material: Wooden case

Notable Features:

  • External speaker or headphones required
  • Tuned circuits: 1 AM circuit
  • Coverage: 180 to 550 meters

Historical Context:

The RCA Radiola III was introduced in 1924 as an affordable and compact radio receiver. It was designed for use with headphones or an external amplifier, making it accessible to a wider audience. The Radiola III utilized the TRF (Tuned Radio Frequency) principle with regeneration, which was a common design in early radio technology. Priced at $35.00, it became popular among radio enthusiasts and helped to further popularize radio listening in the United States.
