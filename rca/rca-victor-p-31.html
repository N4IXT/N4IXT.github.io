RCA Victor P-31 Radio

Years of Production: 1933-1934

The RCA Victor P-31 is a classic tube radio from the early 1930s. It was designed during the golden age of radio, when home entertainment was centered around these devices.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: AM Broadcast Receiver
  • Power Source: AC Mains
  • Tubes: 5 tubes (commonly 6D6, 6C6, 43, 25Z5, and ballast tube)
  • Speaker: Electrodynamic speaker
  • Cabinet: Wooden with a decorative grille

Notable Features:

  • Compact tabletop design
  • Art Deco styling with intricate woodwork
  • Warm tube sound characteristic of the era

Historical Context:

The RCA Victor P-31 was produced during the Great Depression, a time when radios were a vital source of news, entertainment, and connection for families. Its affordability and reliability made it a popular choice for households.

Additional Details:

In the 1940's RCA produced a model P51 (no dash). It was similar in design but is a different radio.

RCA Victor was a leading name in radio technology during this period, and the P-31 exemplifies their commitment to quality and innovation. Today, it is a sought-after collector's item for vintage radio enthusiasts.
