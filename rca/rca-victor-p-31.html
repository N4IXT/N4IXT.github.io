RCA Victor Model P-31 Radio

Years of Production:

1932

Technical Specifications

  • Number of Tubes: 8
  • Tube Types: 34, 30, 32, 34, 30, 34, 30, 30
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne with RF-stage; IF frequency 175 kHz; 2 AF stages
  • Tuned Circuits: 7 AM circuits
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Dry Batteries / 2 x 1.5 & 4 x 45 Volt
  • Loudspeaker: Permanent Magnet Dynamic (PDyn) Loudspeaker (moving coil)
  • Power Output: 0.75 W
  • Material: Leather, canvas, plastic over other material
  • Dimensions: 21.25 x 14.125 x 9.625 inches (540 x 359 x 244 mm)
  • Weight: 14.528 lb (6.596 kg) without batteries
  • Price: $77.00 in the first year of sale

Notable Features

  • Portable Design: The P-31 was designed as a portable set, making it convenient for use without mains power.
  • Class B Amplifier: Utilizes a class B amplifier for output.
  • Similar Models: RCA Victor supplied a very similar radio for the General Electric A81.

Historical Context

The RCA Victor Model P-31 was introduced during the early 1930s, a time when radio technology was rapidly advancing. RCA Victor, a major player in the radio industry, aimed to provide consumers with reliable and portable radio options. The P-31's design reflects the era's emphasis on mobility and accessibility, allowing users to enjoy broadcasts even in areas without mains electricity.

This model is a testament to RCA Victor's innovation and commitment to quality during a period when radio was becoming an essential part of daily life. Its portability and robust design made it a popular choice among consumers, contributing to RCA Victor's reputation as a leading manufacturer of radio equipment.
