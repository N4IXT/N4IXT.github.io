RCA Radiola Super-Heterodyne Radio

Years of Production: 1924

Technical Specifications:

  • Number of Tubes: 6
  • Tube Types: UV199
  • Main Principle: Superheterodyne; ZF/IF 40 kHz; 2 AF stages
  • Wave Bands: Broadcast only (MW)
  • Power Type and Voltage: Dry batteries
  • Dimensions: 21.5 x 36 x 21.5 inches (546 x 914 x 546 mm)
  • Material: Wooden case

Notable Features:

  • External speaker or horn required
  • Tuned circuits: 3 AM circuits
  • Coverage: 180 to 550 meters
  • Incorporated loop antenna in the front door

Historical Context:

The RCA Radiola Super-Heterodyne, also known as the AR-813, was introduced in 1924. It was a significant advancement in radio technology, utilizing the superheterodyne principle for improved selectivity and sensitivity. The radio was designed for use with an external speaker or horn, and it featured a loop antenna integrated into the front door. Priced at $150.00, the Radiola Super-Heterodyne was aimed at radio enthusiasts and early adopters, playing a crucial role in the popularization of radio listening in the United States.
