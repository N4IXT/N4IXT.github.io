Westinghouse WR-13X8 Radio

Years of Production: 1938-1940

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: AM Broadcast Receiver
  • Frequency Range: 540 kHz to 1600 kHz
  • Tubes: 6-tube superheterodyne circuit
  • Power Supply: AC mains
  • Speaker: 6-inch electrodynamic speaker

Notable Features:

  • Art Deco wooden cabinet design
  • Illuminated tuning dial with frequency markings
  • Push-button station presets
  • High-quality audio output for its time

Historical Context:

The Westinghouse WR-13X8 was introduced during the late 1930s, a period when radio was the primary medium for news, entertainment, and music. Its elegant design and reliable performance made it a popular choice for households. The radio's Art Deco styling reflected the design trends of the era, combining functionality with aesthetic appeal.

Additional Details:

This model is considered a collector's item today, valued for its craftsmanship and historical significance. Restored units are often displayed in vintage radio collections and museums.
