RCA Model 100 Speaker

Years of Production: 1925-1928

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Electrodynamic loudspeaker
  • Impedance: High impedance, designed for early tube radios
  • Construction: Metal housing with a decorative grille
  • Dimensions: Approximately 12 inches in diameter

Notable Features:

  • Elegant design with an art-deco style grille
  • Durable construction, built to last
  • Compatible with early RCA and other brand radios

Historical Context:

The RCA Model 100 speaker was introduced during the mid-1920s, a time when radio was becoming a central part of home entertainment. It was one of the first commercially successful loudspeakers designed to work with the early tube radios of the era. Its stylish design made it a popular choice for households, blending functionality with aesthetics.

Additional Details:

The speaker used an electrodynamic driver, which was a significant improvement over earlier horn speakers. This technology provided better sound quality and volume, making it a revolutionary product in its time. The RCA Model 100 is now a sought-after collector's item, valued for its historical significance and unique design.
