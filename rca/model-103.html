RCA Model 103 Speaker

Years of Production: 1926-1930

The RCA Model 103 speaker, also known as the "RCA 103 tapestry speaker," was a popular accessory for early radio enthusiasts during the late 1920s and early 1930s. It was designed to complement RCA's line of radios and provide high-quality sound reproduction.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Electrodynamic speaker
  • Impedance: High impedance, designed for use with early tube radios
  • Frequency Response: Limited by the technology of the era, optimized for voice and music
  • Construction: Decorative wooden frame with a fabric grille featuring intricate tapestry designs

Notable Features:

  • Elegant design with a focus on aesthetics, making it a centerpiece in living rooms
  • Compatible with a wide range of early RCA radios
  • Durable construction, with many surviving examples still functional today

Historical Context:

The RCA Model 103 speaker was introduced during a time when radio was becoming a central part of home entertainment. Its ornate design reflected the Art Deco style popular in the 1920s, and it was marketed as both a functional and decorative piece. The speaker played a significant role in enhancing the listening experience of early radio broadcasts, which included news, music, and entertainment programs.

Additional Details:

The tapestry grille of the RCA Model 103 speaker is one of its most distinctive features, often featuring intricate patterns or scenes. This made the speaker not just a piece of technology but also a work of art, appealing to consumers who valued both form and function.
