Trans Oceanic Model L-600

Years of Production: 1954-1962

The Zenith Trans Oceanic Model L-600 is a highly regarded portable shortwave radio known for its exceptional performance and build quality. It was part of Zenith's iconic Trans Oceanic series, which was designed to provide reliable global radio reception.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: AM broadcast band and multiple shortwave bands
  • Power Source: Operates on AC, DC, or battery power
  • Tubes: Utilizes 6 vacuum tubes for signal amplification
  • Dimensions: Approximately 17 x 11 x 8 inches
  • Weight: Around 17 pounds

Notable Features:

  • Built-in "Wave Magnet" antenna for enhanced reception
  • High-quality sound output with a large speaker
  • Durable and portable design with a leather-covered case
  • Telescoping antenna for improved shortwave reception
  • Dial light for easy tuning in low-light conditions

Historical Context:

The L-600 was introduced during the golden age of shortwave radio, a time when global communication and news relied heavily on radio broadcasts. It was a favorite among travelers, military personnel, and radio enthusiasts due to its ability to receive stations from around the world. The Trans Oceanic series, including the L-600, is often considered a pinnacle of Zenith's engineering and design.

Additional Details:

The L-600's combination of portability, durability, and performance made it a popular choice for decades. Today, it is a sought-after collector's item, admired for its historical significance and craftsmanship.
