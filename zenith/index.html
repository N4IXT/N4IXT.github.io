Overview of Zenith Corporation

Zenith Corporation, founded in 1918 in Chicago, Illinois, was a pioneering American electronics company. Known for its innovation and quality, Zenith played a significant role in shaping the consumer electronics industry, particularly in the field of radio and television manufacturing.

Key Milestones

  • 1918: Zenith was founded by Ralph H. G. Mathews and Karl Hassel.
  • 1923: Introduced the first portable radio.
  • 1933: Developed the first commercial FM radio receiver.
  • 1948: Launched the first subscription television service.
  • 1950s: Introduced the first wireless remote control for TVs, called the "Flash-Matic."

Notable Products

  • Zenith Trans-Oceanic Radios: A series of portable shortwave radios that became iconic for their durability and performance.
  • Zenith Royal 500: One of the first transistor radios, introduced in 1955.
  • Zenith Space Command: The first practical wireless remote control for televisions.

Historical Context

Zenith emerged during a time when radio technology was rapidly evolving. The company capitalized on the growing demand for consumer electronics, particularly during the post-World War II economic boom. Zenith's innovations in FM radio and television technology set industry standards and solidified its reputation as a leader in the field.

Legacy

Although Zenith was eventually acquired by LG Electronics in 1995, its legacy endures through its contributions to the electronics industry. The company's emphasis on innovation and quality left a lasting impact, and its products remain highly regarded by collectors and enthusiasts.
