Trans Oceanic Model B-600

Years of Production: 1954-1962

The Zenith Trans Oceanic Model B-600 was a portable shortwave radio designed for both casual listeners and radio enthusiasts. It was part of the renowned Trans Oceanic series, known for its exceptional performance and build quality.

Technical Specifications:

  • Frequency Coverage: AM broadcast band and multiple shortwave bands
  • Power Source: Operates on both AC power and batteries
  • Tubes: Utilizes 6 vacuum tubes for signal amplification
  • Built-in Antennas: Telescoping whip antenna for shortwave and a loop antenna for AM

Notable Features:

  • High sensitivity and selectivity for long-distance reception
  • Elegant and durable design with a leatherette-covered case
  • Fold-down front panel with a detailed frequency chart
  • Built-in compartment for storing the power cord and accessories

Historical Context:

The B-600 was one of the last tube-based models in the Trans Oceanic series before the transition to transistorized designs. It was a popular choice during its time, offering reliable performance for travelers, military personnel, and radio hobbyists. The radio's ability to receive international broadcasts made it a valuable tool during the Cold War era, providing access to news and information from around the world.

Additional Details:

The Zenith Trans Oceanic Model B-600 remains a sought-after collector's item today, admired for its craftsmanship and historical significance. Restored units are often displayed in museums or cherished by vintage radio enthusiasts.
