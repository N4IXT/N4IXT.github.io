Zenith Model 6-S-229

Years of Production: 1937-1938

The Zenith Model 6-S-229 is a classic tabletop radio from the late 1930s, known for its elegant design and advanced features for the time. It was part of Zenith's "Black Dial" series, which became iconic for its distinctive styling and functionality.

Technical Specifications:

  • Type: Tabletop radio
  • Frequency Bands: AM and Shortwave
  • Tubes: 6-tube superheterodyne circuit
  • Power: AC-powered
  • Speaker: 6-inch electrodynamic speaker

Notable Features:

  • Large black dial with gold accents for easy tuning
  • Wooden cabinet with a rich walnut veneer
  • Push-button station presets
  • High-quality sound output for its era

Historical Context:

The Zenith Model 6-S-229 was produced during a time when radios were a central part of home entertainment. Its design reflects the Art Deco style popular in the 1930s, with sleek lines and a focus on craftsmanship. Zenith was a leader in radio innovation, and the 6-S-229 exemplified their commitment to quality and performance.

Additional Details:

This radio is highly sought after by collectors due to its historical significance and aesthetic appeal. Restored models often serve as functional pieces of history, showcasing the ingenuity of early 20th-century electronics.
